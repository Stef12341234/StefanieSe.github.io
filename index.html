Chicken time

Worldwide there are...

more than 2.000 different chicken breeds. You probably only know the common brown or white chicken on your egg box. However, these are actually hybride chickens. Meaning they are a mix out of many different breeds made to bring the maximum amount of eggs. These chickens actually lay so many eggs that after their laying period they are so used out that they can not even be used for their meat anymore. Due to their high egg production and faster growth they can not probperly build out their bones and are constantly missing vitamins. Therefore many organic farmers go back to using older breeds that can be used for eggs as well as for meat.

One of the breeds that was breeded into the hybride chickens is the New Hampshire chicken. They lay about 220 eggs in their first year and look very similar to the brown chickens that we may be familiar with. The Dutch kind of this breed however has dark tail feathers and some dark around their necks and wings

picture: https://putakputak.com/

Another chicken breed that i would like to mention is the Wyandotte chicken. Their face looks a bit different than the 'common' chickens in that they do not have a big comb and their face is very red with little feathers. Their feather coloring can go from one color to two colors sprinkled in black and white or different browns with white or black. They are very peaceful, social and talkative.
